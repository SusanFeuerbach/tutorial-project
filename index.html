How to Build a Scratching Post for Your Cat

Why build your own cat scratching post?

Scratching is beneficial both physically and emotionally.They need to scratch in order groom their claws. No, they are not "sharpening" their claws. Rather the action of scratching removes the dead layer that builds up on the claws. In addition the action of scratching provides good exercise for the paws, legs and entire body. It is important that the scratcher be tall enough to allow your cat to fully stretch his/her entire length. Cats also scratch for social reasons. Leaving signs of scratching is the cat's version of "tagging". Two things are happening, they are leaving their scents deposited by scent pads in the paws and the actual visual destruction that scratching leaves.

Most commercial scratching posts are not tall enough for the average cat, nor are they stable. One of the worst things that can happen is for a scratcher to fall over while the cat is using it. From the cat's viewpoint this is scary and he/she will not want to use the scratcher. For these reasons it would be best to make your own.

Supplies:

  • Post: 2" x 2"
    Must be untreated wood.
    Can be pine or cedar. (Cats like the smell of cedar.)
    Cut tall enough to allow for kitty's full stretch.

  • Base: 24" plywood circle. 1/2" or 3/4" thick.
    Can be purchased at home centers,craft stores or lumber yards.

  • Top Platform: 12" plywood circle. 1/2".
    Can be purchased at home centers,craft stores or lumber yards.

  • Molding: 1.25" x .25" molding.
    Used for legs under base.

  • Sisal Rope: Two to three packages of 3/8" x 50'
    Do not used treated, dyed or substitute. Will be harmful to the cat.

  • Screws: 4 10 x 3" and 4 8 x 1.75" screws.

  • Paint or Stain:
    To finish base and top platform.

  • Anti-skid pads:
    Everbilt 1 1/2".

Tools:

  • Drill
  • Screw driver
  • Staple Gun & 1/2" staples
  • Glue Gun
  • Wood Glue

Remember to hire the help!

Prep the Parts:

Cut post to appropriate height. For this project the post was cut 33" high.

Sand and finish the base as desired. For this project I stained it black using a water base stain and satin finish. You can also choose to paint the base. Be careful not to make the base too slippery.

You may also choose to finish the top platform. Or you can keep it wood if you plan to cover it.

Warp the sisal rope around the post. Glue the edges of the rope together to prevent unraveling. Staple the starting end to the post using 4-5 staples. Wrap the rope tightly around the post, stapling about every 7-10 rows. Be sure to apply the staples to the same side of the post.

Cut the molding into pieces about 2 1/4" long. Stain or paint to the match the base.

Assemble:

Glue the "legs" evenly distributed around the bottom of the base, about 5/8" from the edge.

Attach the post in the center of the base with the four 3" screws. Attach the top platform to the top of the base with the four shorter screws.

Intice the cat to use

Rub some cat nip into the sisal.

Hang a toy from the top platform.

Place food nearby.

Congradulations!

May you and your cat be happy!
